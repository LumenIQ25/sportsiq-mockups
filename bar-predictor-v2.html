Design Mechanics · Bar predictor active
Same shell as bar trivia — identical 3-column layout, same sidebars. Only the mode badge and center content change.
Mode badge = Predictor — red-tinted pill (distinct from Trivia's purple) in the top bar. Matches the red LIVE indicator and the red-adjacent urgency feel of live-game picks.
Matchup card — real ESPN logos (LAL · GSW), big scores (38px), Q3 · 8:12 LIVE badge. Designed to read across the bar so patrons glancing up instantly know the game state.
Natural-language question — "Will LeBron score in Q3?" — matches the pick_question format in production. TV-sized font (26px scaled; larger on a real 4K set).
Lock timer — amber bar + countdown (0:47). Turns red under 10s (and flashes under 5s, per our earlier rule).
YES / NO pick buttons — 28px bold, full 2-column grid. Green / red to match pick affordances on mobile. No crowd percentages during voting (herd bias rule — same as trivia).
Sharp $ consensus is shown — "Sharp $ 63% YES · market consensus · updates live". This is external betting-market data, not crowd bias. Educational signal, primes bettor instincts, and matches production's mobile predictor display during voting.
Bottom strip localizes — "1,284 picking nationally · 38 at Kincade's" — shows the bar's participation in the national pick.
Reveal/result state (next mockup) — correct outcome highlights the winning button green, shows final crowd %, stat resolution ("LeBron scored 6 pts in Q3"), updates CURRENT GAME leaderboard with winners' +IQ in real time.
